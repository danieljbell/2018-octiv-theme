Typical Project Plan

Click each stage icon to see implementation stage.

  • Week 1: Initiate ~ 1hr

    You’ll receive immediate access to your account, Welcome Kit, and online resources. The Octiv Professional Services team will then conduct a kickoff call with you to understand your specific business needs, set goals for your project, and recommend next steps. You’ll work on gathering initial content and connecting any integrations.

  • Week 2: Design ~ 2hrs

    You’ll consult with the Octiv team to design your end-users’ workflow, the dynamic logic of your documents, and the behavior of your integrations. The Octiv team will provide detailed documentation of the solution we’re building as part of the process.

  • Week 3: Build ~ 5 hrs

    The Octiv team will configure account and integrations settings to your needs and build one to two initial document templates according to our solution design.

  • Week 4: Test ~ 5 hrs

    Octiv will test your solution internally and conduct a testing kickoff with you to demonstrate the document workflow and launch your own testing efforts. During this time, we’ll also conduct live web-based administrative training with your Octiv admin using your own content, so you can help make revisions or start building additional use cases.

  • Week 5: Deploy ~ 2 hr

    We’ll provide initial training resources and best practices for training and deploying to your team. We’ll also support you in migrating your solution to production as needed.

Who's a Good Fit?

  • This typical customer has basic SOWs, Order Forms, Quotes, and/or brief proposals.
  • Already have a commonly used template or very small set of current templates. Typically documents are 1 to 10 pages, with one pricing or quote page with advanced logic.
  • They are using stand-alone or integrated with Salesforce and/or DocuSign./li>
  • They are very technically proficient and want to participate in building/learning.
  • Their value props: efficient doc generation, tracking, integration with Salesforce, and e-sig.

Who's Not a Good Fit?

  • Integration with MS Dynamics or NetSuite
  • Customers with complex internal workflows for collaboration, review, or approval
  • Customers where redlining is primary value prop (are legal users the buyers/users?)
  • Customers where contract lifecycle management (CLM) is primary value prop
  • Customers who have firm and highly-stylized document formatting needs
  • Customers who have larger number of documents and don’t have internal bandwidth to participate in content building
